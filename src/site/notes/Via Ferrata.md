# [[Via Ferrata\|Via Ferrata]]

A **Via Ferrata** is a protected climbing/hiking route mostly found in the [[Alpi\|Alps]]. It require the climber to clip into cables and railings to offer protection and aid in the climb. They can also include extra metal aids, like steps, holds, and ladders.

In the image, some friends on [[Ferrata Passo Stantner\|Ferrata Passo Stantner]] in the [[Dolomiti\|Dolomites]], [[Gruppo del Catinaccio\|Gruppo del Catinaccio]].

## What to carry

‼️ Absolutely necessary:
- Personal protection:
	- [[Kit Ferrata\|Ferrata kit]]
	- [[Imbracatura arrampicata\|Climbing Harness]]
	- [[Caschetto arrampicata\|Climbing helmet]]
	- Hiking boots: a comfortable pair of hiking boots. Harder soles help to have a better grip and provide extra confidence. For full transparency, I've seen people going up ferratas on running shoes, I really do not recommend.
- More necessary things:
	- **Water**: I recommend a water reservoire like a [[Camelbak\|Camelbak]], for easy access without hands, but a water bottle is also ok (especially if travelling with someone else)

Depending on conditions:
Clothing will depend on the current weather conditions. In general:
- Hiking pants
- t-shirt
- puff or other warm layer in case of lower temperatures and/or wind
- Rain/wind jacket (see above)


```ad-warning
collapse: open

Do not go on a Via Ferrata if weather is bad. Rain can make holds slippery, increasing risk. Most importantly, in case of a storm, the railings on the ferrata become lighting rods, putting your life seriusly at risk.

```
